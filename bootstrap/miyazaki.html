Hayao Miyazaki

Personal Information

As a child growing up during WWII, Miyazaki was very familiar with firebombs, air-raid shelters, and especially airplanes, which feature in a lot of his films.

Miyazaki discovered his love for drawing while in school and has since developed his own style of both drawing and storytelling. He is considered one of the best storytellers in all history.

Style

Miyazaki's stories draw a lot from themes such as childhood, anti-war sentiments, and feminism. They depict serious issues in a light-hearted and subtle way.

Notable Films

Miyazaki's most notable film is Spirited Away (2006), for which he won an Oscar.

Random Fact

Most of Miyazaki's characters are based on real people.
